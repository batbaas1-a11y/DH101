# Accessibility

When creating my project, I made intentional choices to ensure it was accessible to a wide range of users. I focused on clarity by using simple, readable language and organizing information in a logical structure so that viewers could easily follow the main ideas. I also paid attention to visual accessibility by choosing clear fonts, sufficient text size, and strong contrast between colors to make the content readable for people with visual impairments.

For my visual elements, such as charts and graphics, I avoided relying solely on color to convey meaning. Instead, I incorporated labels, shapes, and annotations so that the information would still be understandable for individuals with color vision deficiencies. I also made sure that key points were explained in text alongside visuals, which helps users who may have difficulty interpreting graphs or who rely on screen readers.

Overall, I aimed to make my work inclusive and easy to understand, recognizing that accessibility is an important part of effective communication. This process helped me think more critically about how different users might experience my work and how I can design with a broader audience in mind.
