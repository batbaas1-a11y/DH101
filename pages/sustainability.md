# Sustainability & Ethics

### Environmental and Ethical Implications of AI – Reflection

AI is often seen as an invisible digital tool, but it has very real environmental costs. Training and running large models requires massive amounts of energy, water, and physical infrastructure like data centers. This contributes to carbon emissions and resource depletion, especially when AI use scales globally. What makes this more concerning is that most users, including myself, don’t see these impacts directly, which can lead to overuse without considering the consequences.

Ethically, this raises important questions about responsibility and fairness. The environmental burden of AI is not evenly distributed—data centers are often located in regions where energy and water are cheaper, which can place strain on local communities. At the same time, the benefits of AI are often concentrated among large companies and more developed countries. This creates a gap between who benefits and who bears the cost.

Reflecting on this, I think it’s important to be more intentional about how we use AI. While it is a powerful and valuable tool, we should balance innovation with sustainability. This could mean improving efficiency, using renewable energy, and being more mindful of unnecessary usage. As a user, I also have a role in recognizing that even small actions, like frequent AI queries, contribute to a larger environmental impact.
