# Curriculum

In DH101, I learned a broad set of digital humanities skills that connect technology, ethics, and story.

## Core skills

- **AI literacy**: understanding how models generate text and images, and learning to ask smarter, more ethical prompts.
- **Digital storytelling**: crafting narratives with comics, games, articles, and visual media.
- **Text analysis**: using tools like Voyant and GPT to compare machine reading with close human interpretation.
- **Mapping**: visualizing networks, power structures, and geography in digital form.
- **Ethics of AI**: reflecting on labor, bias, consent, transparency, and environmental impact.
- **Website building**: creating a portfolio site with readable content, navigation, and responsive layout.
- **Accessibility**: using descriptive alt text, clear structure, and legible design.
- **Reflection and creative making**: documenting process, decisions, and the role of AI in my work.

## How I learned

This course was about making and thinking together: producing weekly artifacts, writing reflections, and connecting creative practice with critical questions.
