# Week 4 Reflection

Reflect 4: Is AI a collaborator, tool, or plagiarist in storytelling?

AI in storytelling exists in a space between tool, collaborator, and potential plagiarist, depending on how it is used. When AI assists with brainstorming or editing, it functions as a tool that supports human creativity without replacing it. When writers actively interact with AI—selecting, revising, and reshaping its suggestions—it becomes a collaborator in the creative process. However, AI risks becoming a form of plagiarism when its output is presented as original work without meaningful human intervention. Ultimately, authorship and authenticity are not defined by the absence of AI, but by intention, transparency, and creative responsibility. AI may generate possibilities, but it is the human who assigns meaning, direction, and voice to the story.
