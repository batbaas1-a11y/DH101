## Process Notes
How did you make this? 
- I created a speculative future news article set in 2042 using a realistic journalistic style. I started by choosing a current issue—AI training data and lack of transparency—and then imagined what would happen if governments eventually regulated it. I structured the article like a real newspaper piece, including a headline, dateline, quotes, and different perspectives (government, companies, and creators).
What tools did you use?
- I used ChatGPT to help structure the article, and refine the language. I wrote and edited the final version in VS Code.
What decisions did you make?
- I decided to avoid a fully dystopian or utopian future and instead show a mixed outcome. I chose a news article format because it makes the future feel realistic and grounded. I also focused on transparency and data ownership because those are major debates happening today, and I wanted the future to feel like a natural extension of the present.

## Reflection (Artist Statement)
This future presents a mix of both positive and negative outcomes, rather than being purely utopian or dystopian. The Artificial Transparency Act creates more fairness by recognizing the contributions of individuals whose data was used to train AI systems. It gives people more control and makes companies more accountable, which is something currently missing in today’s AI development. However, it also introduces challenges, such as slowing innovation and making it harder for smaller companies to compete due to strict regulations.

This future reveals that, in the present, we are still struggling with questions of data ownership, consent, and power. It shows how much control large tech companies currently have and how little visibility individuals have over how their data is used. The fact that such a law is needed in the future highlights the lack of regulation and accountability today.

To reach this future, governments would need to act more quickly and take AI regulation seriously. Public awareness would also need to increase so that people demand more transparency. On the other hand, to avoid potential negative effects like over-regulation or slowed innovation, there would need to be a balance between ethical responsibility and technological progress.
