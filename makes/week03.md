# Week 3 – Selfie & Identity

## The Artifact
This project analyzes using the Google Ngram Viewer, a tool that creates line-graph visualizations showing how often words or phrases appear in digitized books over time. By typing a word or phrase into the system, the viewer instantly generates a graph from millions of scanned texts, transforming large collections of books into visual representations of language trends.

## Reflection
In this project, “making” is not about producing literary works or interpreting individual texts, but
rather about transforming cultural artifacts into computational objects. “Making” is an activity of
selection, reduction, and visualization. Google Ngram Viewer “makes” one particular variety of
language history in which words are data points and meaning is quantifiable in relation to
frequency. While it is less about interpretation in literary studies, it is still an activity of system
design in choosing what constitutes data, what is excluded, and what kinds of interactions are
enabled with the archive.
In the process of making, when machines are included in the process, there is no particular
person responsible for any particular graph; yet at all levels, there are traces of humans'
decision-making processes. The process of automation helps in faster analysis and the
development of knowledge in various forms, but at the same time, it becomes invisible how the
machine "discovers" trends by implementing its assumptions about language, history, and
legitimacy.
Most obviously, what changes is scale and authority: the ability to work with millions of texts at a
time, but also the risk of obscuring complexity and the presence of bias within this work. Which
changes the work of the humanist from a focus on close reading to a more systemic level,
where the work of critical analysis is to question how the outputs were generated in the first
place.
