# Week 5 – GIF & Remix Culture

## The Artifact
![Nick Young AI Neutral Remix](assets/images/Nick-Young.gif)

## Process Notes
How did you make this? 
- I screen recorded a short portion of the original Nick Young interview clip and trimmed it to about four seconds so the reaction would feel immediate and loop smoothly. I then uploaded the clip to EZGIF, resized it to 480px to meet the assignment requirements, adjusted the frame rate, and converted it into a GIF. After that, I added text in a meme format and ensured the loop played seamlessly before downloading and publishing it to my site.
What tools did you use?
- I used the Mac screen recording tool to capture the clip. I used EZGIF to trim the video, convert it into a GIF, resize it, and add text. I used VS Code to upload the GIF and embed it into my site.
What decisions did you make?
- I decided to keep the clip short so the loop would feel continuous and repetitive. I chose to crop the frame tightly around Nick Young’s facial expression to emphasize the reaction. I selected the text carefully to create a clear contrast between the claim of AI neutrality and the reaction to training data. I also chose a simple meme format instead of adding extra effects so the focus stayed on the message.

## Reflection
This GIF demonstrates how remix culture allows existing media to be recontextualized to create new meaning. The original Nick Young clip is widely recognized as a reaction meme expressing confusion. By adding text referencing AI neutrality and training data, I transformed the clip into commentary on algorithmic bias and the tension between claims of objectivity and the reality of data-driven systems.

The loop is important to the meaning. The repeated confused expression mirrors the ongoing debate about whether artificial intelligence can truly be neutral. AI systems are trained on historical data that often reflects human bias, so the claim of neutrality becomes questionable. The looping format reinforces that this issue is not resolved but continues to surface repeatedly.

Creating this GIF made me think about how short, low-resolution formats can still communicate complex critique. In remix culture, meaning is not fixed in the original content but is reshaped through framing and context. By reusing a familiar meme, I was able to shift its meaning toward a discussion of AI ethics.
