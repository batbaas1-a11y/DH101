# Week 8 Reflection

Reflect 8: Who is visible/invisible in AI networks? How does visualization reveal or obscure power?

AI network visualizations usually make big tech companies, famous researchers, and major institutions visible, especially in places like Silicon Valley. These actors appear central and powerful because they are well-documented and highly connected. However, many important contributors remain invisible—such as data workers, miners, and people in the Global South, as well as environmental impacts. These are often excluded because they are harder to measure or represent. Visualization reveals power by showing who is most connected, but it also obscures power by leaving out hidden labor and inequalities. This means network maps are not neutral—they reflect choices about what (and who) matters.
