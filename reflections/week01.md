# Week 1 Reflection

Reflect 1: What counts as “making” in the age of AI? What might be gained/lost when machines participate in making?

In the age of AI, "making" is less about producing something entirely by hand and more about designing systems and choices- selecting data, setting rules, and guiding machines towards certain outcomes. When machines participate in making, humans often shape the process rather than the final product, which shifts creativity upstream into decisions about inputs, constraints, and interpretation. This can be gained as speed, scale, and new possibilities: AI can reveal patterns, generate ideas, and expand who gets to create. However, "making" with machines also means that we are losing some things, like clear authorship, creativity, and transparency about bias. AI-generated outputs can appear neutral even though they reflect human assumptions built into the system. Making with AI, therefore, becomes a shared act, one that requires critical attention to both human intent and machine influence.
