# Week 5 Reflection

Reflect 5: How does AI alter authorship and remix culture? Who owns AI-made art?

AI changes authorship by blurring who the “creator” really is. When someone uses AI, they guide it with prompts and edits, but the AI generates the output using patterns learned from massive datasets created by other people. The final product is partly human-directed and partly machine-generated, so authorship becomes shared rather than individual.

AI also speeds up remix culture. Art has always borrowed and remixed from the past, but AI does this automatically and at scale. This makes creativity feel more collaborative, but also less transparent.

Ownership is complicated. Legally, users often claim the work, but ethically, it raises questions about the artists whose work trained the AI. AI forces us to rethink creativity as collective rather than purely personal.
